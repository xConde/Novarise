# Tower Aim — Omnidirectional Tower Review

**Branch:** feat/threejs-polish  
**Reviewed:** 2026-04-30 (Phase E sprint 49)

SLOW and CHAIN are field-effect / omnidirectional towers in gameplay fiction but
now yaw their aim subgroup toward the primary target. This review critiques whether
the aim behavior fits the visual identity and documents the decision for each tower.

---

## SLOW tower (emitter dish)

### Fiction
SLOW is a freeze-field emitter: a coil-ring base with a concave energy dish. The
implied mechanism is a cold-energy projection field, not a directed beam. The coil
ring and crystal core are static, implying the field is generated by the whole base,
not by the dish alone.

### Critique
The `slowYaw` wrapper moves only the emitter dish, not the coil ring or crystal.
This is correct partitioning — the dish is the "weapon face" — but a full 360°
rotation breaks the field-weapon read. A SLOW tower directly behind the enemy would
have its dish pointing away from the player, which is visually confusing and fights
the "you can see what it's doing" design goal.

### Decision: SHIP amplitude clamp — ±90° arc

The emitter dish now rotates within ±90° (π/2 radians) of forward. The result:
- When the primary target is in the front 180° arc, the dish tracks it exactly.
- When the primary target is behind the tower, the dish clamps to the nearest side
  (±90°), giving a clear "leaning sideways" read while still indicating the target
  hemisphere.
- The coil ring and crystal core remain static, reinforcing the "field base" read.

**Verdict:** Acceptable as-is with the clamp. The aim gesture reads as "the emitter
head is oriented toward its target" without breaking the omnidirectional field fiction.

---

## CHAIN tower (sphere + electrodes)

### Fiction
CHAIN is a Tesla coil: a static coil base with a floating sphere and radial
electrodes. The arc discharge jumps to the nearest valid target in any direction —
the whole tower is the weapon, not a directed barrel.

### Critique
`chainYaw` moves the sphere, all 4 electrodes, the arc mesh, and the T2/T3 orbit
spheres. The coil base stays static. The effect: the electrode cluster "tilts" to
face the primary target.

Full 360° rotation: the electrode cluster pointing directly at the target makes
the tower look like a directional weapon. The orbit spheres (Finding B-4) tilt
with the yaw, which is cosmetically acceptable but reinforces the directional read.

### Decision: SHIP amplitude clamp — ±90° arc

Same clamp as SLOW. The electrode cluster leans toward the target (front 180°) or
holds at the nearest side (±90° for targets behind). The arc discharge VFX is still
omnidirectional, so the "lightning strikes anywhere" fiction is maintained even
though the electrode cluster has a preferred orientation.

**Verdict:** Acceptable as-is with the clamp. The B-4 orbit tilt is a cosmetic
issue (tilt is plausible for orbiting charges that "lean" toward the output arc)
and remains deferred — see STRATEGIC_AUDIT.md Finding B-4.

---

## SPLASH tower (drum cluster)

### Critique
SPLASH is a rocket launcher cluster. The drum + tubes are a directional weapon
(rockets fly in the direction the barrel points). Full 360° yaw on `splashYaw` is
correct — the drum physically needs to face the target for the fire animation to
make sense. No clamp needed.

**Verdict:** No change. Full rotation is correct for SPLASH.

---

## BASIC, SNIPER, MORTAR

These are unambiguously directional weapons. Full rotation is correct and expected.
No clamp.

---

## C-3 deferral: STRONGEST/WEAKEST damage lag

The aim subsystem does not recompute on `damageEnemy` calls. For STRONGEST/WEAKEST
targeting modes, a tower may aim at an enemy whose health changed mid-combat (lowering
it from first to second place). The tower will aim at the now-second-strongest for up
to one turn. 

Assessment: this requires adding `'damage'` to the `EnemyService.enemiesChanged`
Subject type AND a gated invalidation path in `TargetPreviewService` that only
bulk-dirties on damage events when STRONGEST/WEAKEST towers are present. Estimated
scope: ~50-70 lines across 3 files + new specs. The visual artifact is one-turn (the
tower fires at the correct target anyway — only the aim indicator is stale). Cost
exceeds benefit for Phase E. **Deferred.**

## Constants added

`AIM_AMPLITUDE_CONFIG` in `tower-aim.constants.ts`:

| Tower | Amplitude | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| BASIC | π (unrestricted) | Directional barrel — full rotation correct |
| SNIPER | π (unrestricted) | Directional barrel — full rotation correct |
| SPLASH | π (unrestricted) | Directional drum cluster — full rotation correct |
| SLOW | π/2 (±90°) | Field weapon — dish leans, doesn't spin |
| CHAIN | π/2 (±90°) | Field weapon — electrode tilts, doesn't spin |
| MORTAR | π (unrestricted) | Directional barrel — full rotation correct |

The clamp is applied in `tickAim` before `lerpYaw`, so the short-path lerp still
operates within the clamped range. Specs in `tower-animation.service.spec.ts` verify
the clamp fires correctly for SLOW and CHAIN and is a no-op for BASIC and MORTAR.
